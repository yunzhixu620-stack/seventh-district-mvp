# Experience Direction

## V3 Positioning

**中文：** 一场真人与 AI 混入的都市 RP 夜局：你以社会身份入场，为自己的目标行动，在结算前判断谁在利用你、谁可能根本不是人。

**English:** An urban social-roleplay night session where human-like and AI-controlled slots mingle unseen; play your agenda, shift the street and judge who has been playing you.

V3 is a single-player pseudo-multiplayer experience. Linxia's disappearance and the circulating envelope are a contested social event, not merely a detective puzzle to solve.

## Target Fantasy

The player should feel:

- I have a social identity, leverage, fear and personal win condition.
- The other participants pursue agendas without waiting for my prompts.
- I can influence what the street believes, not just discover facts.
- I cannot tell during play whether a role is human-like, AI-controlled, fixed or systemic.
- The report reveals what happened behind the performances.

## Experience Pillars

1. **Roleplay before investigation.** Each selected role changes what the player wants, risks and can profitably do.
2. **Social freedom with deterministic consequences.** Buttons and free text become ruled intentions; canon and results never come from generated prose.
3. **Hidden AI participants with agency.** AI-controlled roles may approach, trade, mislead, shield, expose or suppress, while their control type stays concealed in play.
4. **A readable street reaction.** Heat, administrator attention, evidence risk, public opinion and public/private channels make the social contest legible.
5. **Reveal is the payoff.** The report exposes controller types, variable truth, role goal outcomes and who successfully misled the player.

## Why AI Is Necessary

AI is not a dialogue badge and should not be advertised inside the session. Its value is that role slots can respond to unusual player tactics while also behaving like self-interested participants. Deterministic engines select legal actions and effects; an LLM may phrase permitted messages, but cannot decide canon, identities or outcomes.

## What V3 Is Not

- **Not a GTA physical sandbox:** the MVP models social action and street pressure, not driving, combat, 3D traversal or physics.
- **Not a visual-novel Q&A demo:** characters do not exist only to answer the player, and AI identity is not shown during play.
- **Not a fixed detective puzzle:** the envelope can be leveraged, hidden, protected, traded or exposed under variable truths and role goals.

## Visual And Audio Direction

- Retain the night-city atmosphere and original assets where useful, but shift the interface toward a live street lobby: participants, channels, location activity and state pressure.
- Copy uses the supplied V3 narrative bible as source of truth: concrete, short, suspicious and driven by desire or fear.
- Ambient and feedback audio remains opt-in and legally self-contained unless a future reviewed stage introduces licensed assets.

## V3 Boundaries

- Keep Linxia Envelope, variable truth, low-violence suspense, deterministic ruling and rule-parsed free speech.
- Keep LLM phrasing constrained behind structured context and consistency protection.
- Do not add true multiplayer, account systems, 3D/combat, public UGC authoring or paywalls during the V3 MVP refactor.
- Do not reveal which roles are AI, fixed NPCs, system proxies or future human seats until the post-game report.
