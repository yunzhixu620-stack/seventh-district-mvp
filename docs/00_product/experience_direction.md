# Experience Direction

## Positioning

**中文：** 一局可重玩的都市身份博弈：你用言辞、伪装与选择穿过不可靠的信息，决定一封信该被谁看见。

**English:** A replayable urban identity game where persuasion, disguise, and imperfect information determine who should see one envelope.

Target players enjoy narrative investigation and social deduction, but want a short solo browser session with visible consequence rather than open-ended chat.

## Experience Pillars

1. **Motivated people, not clue dispensers.** Every slot wants something and may mislead the player.
2. **Freedom with rulings.** Recommendations lower entry friction; free text adds expression; deterministic rules retain fairness.
3. **A readable conspiracy.** Public noise, private pressure, known clues, and exposure risk must remain scannable.
4. **Replay reveals the system.** A final report surfaces truth, missed clues, and goal outcomes without erasing uncertainty during play.

## Visual And Audio Direction

- Night-city palette: ink background, slate panels, warm envelope gold, signal cyan, warning coral.
- Typography and copy favor terse incident-report rhythm paired with personal messages.
- Sound events: subdued street ambience when enabled; subtle receipt cue for messages; brighter discovery cue for clues; warning pulse for high exposure; restrained conclusion tone.
- Audio ships as original procedural Web Audio cues and synthesized ambience, avoiding external asset licensing risk.

## MVP Boundaries

No real LLM, online multiplayer, violence-centered episode, 3D world, voice recognition, account system, or UGC editing.
