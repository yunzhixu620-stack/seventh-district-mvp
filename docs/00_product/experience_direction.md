# Experience Direction

## V3 Positioning

**中文：** 一场真人与 AI 混入的都市 RP 夜局：你以社会身份入场，
为自己的目标行动，在结算前判断谁在利用你、谁可能根本不是人。

**English:** An urban social-roleplay night session where human-like and
AI-controlled slots mingle unseen. Play your agenda, shift the street and
judge who has been playing you.

V3 is a single-player pseudo-multiplayer experience. Linxia's disappearance
and the circulating envelope are a contested social event, not merely a
detective puzzle to solve.

## Target Fantasy

The player should feel:

- I have a social identity, leverage, fear and personal win condition.
- Other participants pursue agendas without waiting for my prompts.
- I can influence what the street believes, not just discover facts.
- I cannot tell in play whether a role is human-like, AI-controlled, fixed or
  systemic.
- The report reveals what happened behind the performances.

## Experience Pillars

1. **Roleplay before investigation.**
   Each selected role changes what the player wants, risks and can
   profitably do.
2. **Social freedom with deterministic consequences.**
   Buttons and free text become ruled intentions. Canon and results never
   come from generated prose.
3. **Hidden AI participants with agency.**
   AI-controlled roles may approach, trade, mislead, shield, expose or
   suppress while their control type stays concealed in play.
4. **A readable street reaction.**
   Heat, administrator attention, evidence risk, public opinion and channels
   make the social contest legible.
5. **Reveal is the payoff.**
   The report exposes controller types, variable truth, role goal outcomes
   and who successfully misled the player.

## Why AI Is Necessary

AI is not a dialogue badge and should not be advertised inside the session.
Its value is that role slots can respond to unusual player tactics while also
behaving like self-interested participants.

Deterministic engines select legal actions and effects. An LLM may phrase
permitted messages, but it cannot decide canon, identities or outcomes.

## What V3 Is Not

- **Not a GTA physical sandbox.**
  The MVP models social action and street pressure, not driving, combat,
  three-dimensional traversal or physics.
- **Not a visual-novel question-and-answer demo.**
  Characters do not exist only to answer the player, and AI identity is not
  shown during play.
- **Not a fixed detective puzzle.**
  The envelope can be leveraged, hidden, protected, traded or exposed under
  variable truths and role goals.

## Visual and Audio Direction

- Retain the night-city atmosphere and original assets where useful, but
  shift the interface toward a live street lobby: participants, channels,
  location activity and state pressure.
- Copy uses the supplied V3 narrative bible as source of truth: concrete,
  short, suspicious and driven by desire or fear.
- Ambient and feedback audio remains opt-in and legally self-contained unless
  a later reviewed stage introduces licensed assets.

## V3 Boundaries

- Keep Linxia Envelope, variable truth, low-violence suspense, deterministic
  ruling and rule-parsed free speech.
- Keep LLM phrasing constrained behind structured context and consistency
  protection.
- Do not add true multiplayer, accounts, three-dimensional combat, public
  user-generated authoring or paywalls during the V3 MVP refactor.
- Do not reveal which roles are AI, fixed NPCs, system proxies or future
  human seats until the post-game report.

## Narrative Source of Truth

The owner-provided V3 narrative bible controls key story text, role motives,
variable truths, pivotal messages and endings for this refactor.

The narrative/data stage must import that source before UI implementation. It
may structure or shorten content for display, but it must not casually
rewrite its meaning.
