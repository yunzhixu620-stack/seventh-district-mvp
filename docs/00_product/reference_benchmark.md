# Reference Benchmark: Interaction Lessons

This is an implementation guide, not a claim of feature parity. We borrow design principles and create original content, interface styling, and audio.

| Reference | Useful principle | Seventh District implementation | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Retail Mage](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3224380/Retail_Mage/) | Free intent becomes system-resolved consequences. | Typed or recommended actions receive a visible parsing preview and deterministic ruling outcome. | No generated world simulation or copied humor/assets. |
| [Suck Up!](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2726370/Suck_Up/) | Identity performance creates trust and exposure stakes. | Disguise and trade alter risk, resources, and message responses. | No voice capture, vampire premise, or 3D imitation. |
| [Dead Meat](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2628740/Dead_Meat/) | Open interrogation feels expressive when reactions matter. | Ask and probe accept free text but reveal only allowable authored clues. | No murder-focused content or copied dialogue. |
| [Blood on the Clocktower](https://www.bloodontheclocktower.com/) | Asymmetric information and motivated roles produce replayable social deduction. | Six role slots, private/public channels, seed-driven truth and replay report. | No character art, terminology, or board layout replication. |

## Decision Checks

- Copy: restrained urban suspense; actionable, short choices; uncertain motives rather than violence.
- UI: a three-pane situation board keeps identity, events, and intelligence legible; it intentionally avoids chatbot-only presentation.
- Interaction: every meaningful action exposes likely cost and risk before confirmation.
- Sound: ambient urban bed plus semantic cues; no licensed soundtrack dependency.
- Validation: each benchmark lesson maps to observable playable behavior in the QA checklist.

Sources accessed May 25, 2026. Product pages may change over time.
